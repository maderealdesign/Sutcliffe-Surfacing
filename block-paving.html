Paving Detail

Good paving is all about the groundwork you do not see.

Block paving and flagging give a property structure, pattern and definition. They can frame a driveway, create practical garden paths, build a smart patio or form a hard-wearing entrance with more character than a plain surface.

We plan the levels, sub-base, edge restraints and drainage from the start, because those details decide whether paving continues to look neat after real daily use. Without a rock-solid foundation, even the most beautiful paving will ultimately fail.

Why It Matters

Built for how the space is used

Accurate Levels

Levels and falls are planned so the paving works seamlessly with doors, drains, paths, walls and existing garden features. Proper leveling prevents water pooling and ensures longevity.

Strong Restraints

Edges, kerbs and borders are carefully specified to keep the surface strictly contained and tidy over time. Without strong edge restraints, block paving can shift under vehicular weight.

Pattern & Texture

Paving can add significant visual rhythm through bond pattern, colour mix, border choice and the varying size of slab or block. This customization ensures a completely unique aesthetic.

Helpful Answers

Common questions

Is block paving suitable for driveways?

Yes, when it is installed on the right sub-base with suitable edge restraints and a layout designed specifically for heavy vehicle use. The deep compacted base is what provides the structural strength.

Can paving be combined with resin or tarmac?

Yes. Paving borders and detail areas can work very well alongside resin bound or tarmac surfaces. It often provides a fantastic contrasting frame that highlights the main driveway surface.

Do you handle drainage and levels?

Yes. Drainage, falls and property thresholds are rigorously assessed before the final paving layout is finalised. Ensuring water drains away from your home correctly is paramount to our installation process.
